# Motion-quality reference: principles, not a template

The strong internal reference succeeds mainly because important early states
physically or causally become the next state: clutter collapses into a focal
object, clicks reveal consequential product states, a shared environment carries
through product setup, measured geometry docks a recurring object, and the one
large camera move follows a semantic focal change. Outgoing and incoming
information overlap, gestures anticipate/action/settle/hold, and the close has a
readable tableau.

It is not perfect: one middle boundary is an abrupt editorial reset, some later
connections are dissolves, repeated marks are separate instances rather than
literal shared elements, and its landed camera holds are short. Treat those as
useful cautions.

Extract continuity, causal interaction, measured placement, motivated lens
movement, energy hierarchy, and rest. Do not copy white cards, its palette,
product UI, copy, shot order, logo assembly, typewriter behavior, or camera shot.
A motivated hard cut is valid. Do not maximize motion density or require a morph
at every boundary.
