# outgoing-morph-seam — resolved surface, motivated compression, handoff pill

**What it is.** A source surface with a clear result state, followed by a
purpose-built outgoing phrase. Its product detail releases first, the surface
compresses into a rounded pill, the surviving outcome label appears, and the
pill continues toward the next shot. The movement begins before the boundary,
so a subsequent shape-match reads as the consequence of the product action
rather than an ornamental transition pasted onto two static frames.

**When to declare it.** Use for an outgoing morph seam, shape-match handoff,
continuity handoff, or match cut whose source can resolve into a pill. Its
defaults are complete for host auto-declaration. Set `bridgePart` to the exact
outgoing cut endpoint and use the same value as the scene cut's `fromPart`;
the incoming shot should expose a similarly proportioned pill or compact card.

**How to stage the shot around it.** Keep the camera settled during the final
0.5 seconds. Put the boundary 0.35–0.7 seconds after `handoffSec`, while the
pill is still carrying gentle directional momentum. Do not animate the scene
wrapper in competition with the cut runtime. The detailed product result is a
PRIMARY moment before the compression; the landed pill is transition
paperwork, not a new information beat.

**Slots.** `eyebrow`, `title`, and `body` describe the source result;
`handoffLabel` survives the compression; `bridgePart` is the stable cut
endpoint; `accent` is a brand token; `handoffSec` is when the compressed pill
lands before the cut.
