# ambient-hero-opener — stable hero, living depth field, finite ambience

**What it is.** A first-shot product stage whose focal app surface and copy
assemble once, settle cleanly, then stay optically stable while the *world
behind them* remains alive. A wallpaper lattice drifts slowly, two soft depth
objects counter-move, and the key light breathes over a finite timeline. The
motion belongs to imagery and atmosphere, never to the reading block. This is
the reusable answer to the long parked frame that feels like a slide.

**When to declare it.** Use for an ambient hero, cinematic opener, living
canvas, or product-first opening shot. It is strongest in the first scene of a
film whose opening claim needs two or more seconds of reading room. Its
defaults are deliberately complete, so a high-confidence retrieval can be
host-declared without inventing copy.

**How to stage the shot around it.** Keep the camera locked or use one gentle
push that lands before `settleSec`; the recipe already supplies depth motion.
Do not place another moving text block over it. The product stage fills most
of the frame and owns the eye; a following directional cut may leave after
the hero has held for at least one second. Declare the settled hero as a
PRIMARY moment at roughly `sceneStart + settleSec`.

**Slots.** `eyebrow`, `headline`, and `body` own the stable reading block;
`metric` and `metricLabel` populate the compact product proof; `accent` is a
brand token; `settleSec` ends the assembly; `ambientSec` is the finite ambient
drift duration and should stay inside the scene.
