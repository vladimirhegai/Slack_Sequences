# overlap-dashboard-entrance — one assembly grammar, tightly overlapped

**What it is.** A substantial product dashboard that arrives as one system,
not a pile of independent fades. The shell rises first; its navigation rail
follows 80ms later; three metric cards and the chart enter on a compact
left-to-right chain; activity rows settle last. Every phrase begins before
the lead finishes and uses the same rise/assemble grammar. Chart bars then
grow as the final payoff, giving the shot development after the entrance.

**When to declare it.** Use when a brief explicitly asks for an overlapping
dashboard entrance, staggered product UI assembly, metrics cascading into a
dashboard, or follow-through across a product surface. Prefer the
`dashboard-grid` plugin when the request is merely “show a dashboard”; this
recipe is for the *specific choreography* where the assembly itself is the
shot.

**How to stage the shot around it.** Let the recipe own the first 2–3 seconds
of the scene. A calm push-in may land with the final chart bar, but avoid a
whip during the overlap chain. The dashboard is already frame-filling; do not
place a second hero beside it. Declare a PRIMARY moment at roughly
`sceneStart + settleSec`, then leave at least one second to read the landed
system.

**Slots.** `product` names the workspace, `title` names the view,
`metric1..3` are compact proof values, `accent` is a brand token, and
`settleSec` is the final chart-bar landing.
